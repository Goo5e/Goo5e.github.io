Parallel Systems

The general concept of running multiple calculations of a program at a time is known as parallel computing, it gives a boost in processing speed by splitting a complex problem into many smaller ones that can be handled separately at the same time. This idea is not limited to use in GPUs and a parallel system may be run by multiple processors within a computer, or even multiple devices across a network, anything where the workload can be split without depending on the other outputs.

Advantages

  • This process can increase speeds of parallel tasks to a great degree.
  • The system can output more data per cycle.
Disadvantages
  • Parallel systems are usually harder to program.
  • They are usually more power-intensive than non-parallel alternatives.
  • They cannot speed up (or could even slow down) tasks that are not parallel.
